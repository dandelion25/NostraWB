


The stiltship was first developed from the floating homes of Medic brine farmers, who built pavilions and working decks supported on a mix of barrels, cork floats, and reed bundles. Rough waves and seepage would frequently swamp these platforms over time, and it had become common practice by the 4th century for these homes to be raised on stilts - common wisdom noted the superior stability of platforms that had sunk so low as to be fully submerged, and veteran brine farmers grew skilled in managing waterlogged rafts that could approach particularly choppy waters. Noted as little more than a regional curiosity for centuries, they were catapulted into naval relevance during the conquests of the short-lived Nagsidayan dynasty in the early 500s. Unwilling to face Eiyanixie in the mountains, the Nagsid forces planned a surprise naval invasion of Mediya, taking advantage of the weakened Yeoxli navies to cut apart the Eiyan army in a series of pincer movements supplied by sea.
