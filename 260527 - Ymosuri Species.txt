*Species Entry : Ymosuri*

-- * --

Spread across much of what is now the Nexus, the Ymosuri are a species of upright, bushy-tailed, plantigrade quadrupeds capable of comfortable habitation from 8 to 27 C in dry conditions, 15 to 30 C when wet, depending on the grown length and phenotypically-governed density of their coarse fur. Sublethal cold from 0 to 15 C (external, not IBT) will induce torpor without impacting overall health beyond some personality abnormalities and temporary loss of smell. They are omnivorous, preferring a diet of starches, sweets, mushrooms, and meats, and are capable of digesting chitin; nutritionally, they require substantial quantities of both calcium and vitamins A and D, which they cannot synthesize self-sufficiently under any conditions.

Ymosuri height typically varies between 140 and 170 cm, corresponding to average weights of 40 and 50 kg, respectively. Their sex is four-chromosome bimodal with minimally dimorphic male and female sexes, both of which can produce milk from four teats. Despite being apparently mammalian, Ymosuri lay and hatch from large, elongated ~4.5-5 kg leathery eggs after 10 to 22 months (laid after 2 months), depending on temperature. With the advent of controlled heating, cooling, and humidity control, however, the lower bound has become more common in practice.
