1500 kW Steam Aircraft Powerplant Profile

Overview:

Shaft Power Output: 1500 kW

Thermal Input: ~6000 kW via pulse combustion

Cycle Efficiency: ~25% (Rankine cycle with superheat)

Application: Heavy-lift flying boat, pulse-combustion based monotube steam turbine


System Breakdown:

1. Pulse Combustion Firebox (~1000–1300 kg)

Multi-chamber, 6000 kW thermal output

Tuned for superheating monotube boiler coils



2. Monotube Boiler (~700–900 kg)

High-flow, folded spiral configuration for fast heat exchange



3. Steam Turbine + Gearbox (~600–800 kg)

Output: 1500 kW shaft power

Based on 1930s naval turbine weight/power benchmarks



4. Condenser System (~1000–1300 kg)

Wing-integrated fin radiators for 4500 kW rejection


5. Pumps, Feedwater & Control (~250 kg)


6. Structure & Insulation (~300–500 kg)



Total Powerplant Mass: ~3850–4780 kg
Specific Output: ~0.31–0.39 kW/kg

Validation:

Steam turbines in 1930s reached 0.3–0.4 kW/kg (marine/rail)

Rankine cycle with superheat routinely achieved 22–28%

Wing-based aluminum fin condensers structurally viable

Monotube boilers used in naval & vehicle applications


Conclusion:
This configuration is historically plausible with speculative yet 
grounded advances in pulse combustion and superheat utilization.


Addendum(human): firebox weight & performance was interpolated between
90-95% efficiency pulse combustion boilers and the buzz bomb engine.
Superheated steam system, most weight savings that may be from anachronistic
alloy assumptions are in piping, condenser, and feed systems, which can
be improved using prometheum and made corrosion-resistant by using rare
