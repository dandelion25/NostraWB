Troublemakers of the Siayeo Nexus

**Heathen agitators**
There are always those opposed to the influence and reach of the Siayeo clergy, and their pervasive control over many aspects of life. Borne from disputes over political autonomy, land use, religious freedom, foreign influence, and taxes, they are prevalent in the Nexus' associate states - particularly in the west, where integration is more recent and businesses have foreign ties. 

**An Ze Insurgents**
A sovereign nation since the late decline of Greater Yeoxli, An Ze was subjugated by Eiyanixie in the mid-900s and, among other concessions, made to relinquish its lucrative prometheum mines. Discontent is rampant, and insurgent witches stalk its forests; though largely isolated by the associate state of Hziaz Qolan, mining operations continue to be disrupted across the territory. Stolen prometheum finds its way into black markets across the western coast, funding the resistance and supplying illicit magical efforts worldwide.

**Miadnyan**
Though now formally a supervised state of Eiyanixie with the conclusion of the Medyanhi Wars, the Miadnyayan Duchy was first recognised by the Empire of Nya following the opportunistic conquests of the eccentric warlord queen **Myhryanss Kote**, who led a portion of her great-aunt's personal army to sieze control of had been the most densely-trafficked route for black market prometheum in the Siayeo Nexus. Using the influx of cash to great effect, she ensured her duchy would suffer no threat to its violent new hierarchy - not the beleagured Medyanhi regulars, outmatched by her well-paid mercenaries, and not Eiyanixie, held at a distance through careful maneuvers in the twisting trenches of Siayeo-Nyayanese diplomatic relations.

Though she no longer engages openly in piratical actions, she is a frustrating wildcard to many a local diplomat, and a regular in-between for politicians seeking underworld contacts. For all her existence is begrudgingly accepted, few hesitate to label her as a troublemaker in the context of law and order within the Nexus.

**Tiaremed Fundamentalists**
The pacification of the Tiaremed states was not without survivors - there are those who continue to practice the old traditions, and others still who spurn anew the Siayeo-syncretic practices of modern, moderate Tiaremed socialism. Though far less active now than they were at the height of the Navarin Socialist Republic, there remain those elements who seek a return to the practices of the Revolution, some more militant than others; theirs is a common banner taken up by socioeconomic discontents, or impressed upon them as rebellions entrench under pressure.
