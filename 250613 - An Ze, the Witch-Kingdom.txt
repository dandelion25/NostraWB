Feature : **An Ze, the Witch-Kingdom**
-- * --

Prometheum was first discovered in An Ze in the early 2nd century, its uses kept secret for centuries by its artificers and witch-kings. Their magical power made them practically unassailable, from the Yeoxli Empire through to the modern era - only with the advent of heavy artillery did they finally fall, crushed beneath the overwhelming might of the Nexus. The witches of An Ze are extremely effective auxiliaries, when recruited - so too are their insurgents, and the chaos they are capable of.

The particular brand of magic used by An Ze would be considered excessively extravagant elsewhere. Blessed with a natural abundance of prometheum, their artificers had little issue with its expenditure. Zeian magics are powered by the magical charging and controlled detonation of prometheum, formed of an unblemished crystalline core and a thin, rune-forged metallic shell. This rapidly entropizes the magic flowing within, generating a powerful surge that can be chained and amplified through careful geometric arrangement. These constructs are typically used to used to boost the power of witch-spells, but can be crafted into potent battlefield weapons in their own right. The art is far from lost, but rarely practiced today; prometheum has far too many uses to be affordable for a Zeian-style thaumaturge in the wider world.

An Ze's mines supply a huge portion of the Nexus's prometheum, consisting up to a third of domestic consumption. They have been developed extensively since their capture by Eiyanixie, combining native Zeian artifice with Siayeo technology and sacred hydrothaumaturgy to excavate ever deeper - so intensive is their activity, that the limits of production are imposed by the throughput of its elevators and the risk of prometheum detonation. Even now they continue to expand: prometheum demand is always rising, and new sources are always sought.
