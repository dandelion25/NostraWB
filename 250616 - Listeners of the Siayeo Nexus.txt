Feature : Listeners of the Siayeo Nexus

The Listeners are the highest and innermost circle of the Siayeo priesthood, taking on the byzantine task of interpreting the vast will of the Great Tree. Theirs is the word to which all the Siayeo temples are ultimately subordinate, carefully crafted from the countless logs and reports passed upwards from the Siayeo Adepts - those who have the duty of not only caring for the Root, but monitoring its changes for this purpose. The Listeners carry a great moral burden; the Great Tree has learned much of mortal life, but mortal it is not, nor does it necessarily partake in mortal ideals of what is good or just. One branch may die off for the sake of another, just as sinkholes might devastate a town for a fleeting, fickle purpose. It is the role of the Listeners to ensure this does not happen - to deploy the might of the Nexus in anticipation, or to succeed in convincing the Great Tree itself.

The Listeners are often eccentric and severe in their habit. Theirs is a world more of the ritual than the political, heavily reliant on the advice of Adepts for knowledge of worldly affairs. Some among them can come across as conceited and self-righteous, high on the prestige and honour of being directly attendant to the Great Tree. In the end, however, it is a poor Listener who cannot conciliate the agendas of the Nexus' manifold and wilful, balancing the needs of the mortal and the divine; humility is a virtue quickly learned among them, for beyond its convenience as an affectation, it is the ability to be adaptable and open-minded that best serves their ultimate purpose.
