Once a single tree on the outskirts of Eiyanysie, the Great Tree first became known when it breached the city's waterworks and began manipulating the flow of stormwater across several districts. Initially slated for removal, it quickly became the focus of several small cults, gaining protected status around 1300 years ago as a local god of the city's tertiary garden tier. Of these cults, the Mohgir were first to discover the Essential Breath, the medium of the Great Tree's influence: fluid drawn uninterrupted from the peripheral branches would exhibit esoteric properties far greater potency than that of water poured over its leaves, the dominant ritual at the time. The Siayeo, a divergent sect of the Mohgir, would go on to develop an understanding of Breath that enabled construction of the Root, the medium through which the Great Tree inhabits the Siayeo Nexus.

Though revered as a sacred aspect, the Essential Breath is of significant practical utility: for it is, among other aspects, the nature of the Great Tree to radiate, reflected in the promotion of emanating flow across the Root. The strength of this effect varies with proximity, Breath, and crucially, the attention of the Great Tree: while based in natural affect, there exist ritual and material means to invoke its aid and attention wherever the Root draws Breath. Such means have been incorporated into a vast array of functions, ranging across excavation, transport, weather control, weaponry, and even telegraphy - many having become possible with the advent of steam power, mechanisation, and modern ideas.

For all the Great Tree represents, it is still a physical being. Its power alone, estimable by the productivity of its grove (~493 km2, theorised work capacity ~14 GW), is well below the level necessary to distribute Breath across the Root; the vast majority of the Nexus' power is supplied by the Wells and Siayeo Adepts, without which the Root would rapidly stagnate.
