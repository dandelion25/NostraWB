**Leaders of the Nexus - Basic Bios**
-- --

**Miayan - King Jaiho VI**
Born prince Ojenji Jaiho, the sixth king of Miayan was coronated in 1017 following the formal withdrawal of his mother from the throne. His first acts were to pass a series of legal reforms, among them the targeted curtailing of the death penalty. Though a capable civilian administrator, he often clashes with his admirals on matters of budgeting and diplomatic commitments. Jaiho VI is known to be affable, easygoing, and persuasive - he has led Miayan through numerous trends in enlightened management, opening the way for his socioeconomic agendas. He holds Tim of the CNIN in high regard, and is an avid reader of individualist psychology.

**Yineng - President Kalluajigk**
Magkiit Kalluajigk was born the daughter of a lumberjack and a preacher. She joined the army in 1022, rising to the position of company commander; she was given the moniker 'Mother Banshee' while leading her troops by the sound of her hollers through an ambush in pitch darkness during the Medyanh Intervention. Kalluajigk entered politics as an advisor to her local representative, eventually splitting off after a series of disagreements and an aborted sabre duel. Her faction gathered the support of the military and several large industrial concerns, but most were reluctant to empower her agendas of intensified industrial collectivisation and pro-expeditionary policy, too similar to those of the radical Tiaremedist coups that had erupted decades prior. Kalluajigk's election is attributed by some to the endorsement of King Jaiho, who had met personally with Kalluajigk in 1029.

**Khivya - Prime Minister Omhoka**
Born to a respected banking family, Omhoka was active in politics from a young age. Frustrated with the stagnant economy, they cooperated briefly with a cell of radical Tiaremedists before growing disillusioned with the cause, retaining their connections but ultimately returning to the traditions of their family's practice. Splitting from the radical party, Omhoka led several moderate coalitions on the platform of economic reform - elected once in 1014 and a second time in 1029, Omhoka is commonly credited with the modernisation of the Khivyan National Bank and the formal establishment of the Khivyan stock exchange. Their indifference to exploitation abroad and easing of tariffs on Nyayanese imports has drawn the ire of ethicist organisations.

**Eiyanixie - Chancellor Siuahnu**
Htshepsa Siuahnu apprenticed under the Siayeo Adepts for six years, working briefly in supply before relenquishing her place to engage in secular politics. She joined the National Liberal Reform Coalition in 1022; following the eruption of the Medyanh Civil War and the ensuing economic disruptions, she was instrumental in harnessing public opinion to bolster the party's influence, rising to the position of party leader amidst the exposure of a series of embezzling and extramarital scandals. in just two years following her appointment, she successfully orchestrated the integration of Miadnyaya and the alliance of Hziaz Qolan in cooperation with Consul Temie and Mihryans Kote.

**Anggayan - Consul-Executive Temie**
Yeyan Temie was born daughter to a secretary of the provincial Censor, and apprenticed under several police bureaus before entering the secret service. Following her retirement in 1011, she was appointed the aide of a senior senator before rising to the Senate herself, furthering her political career on anti-corruption rhetoric and the expansion of coastal security measures. Though active for over a decade, her faction did not become prominent until the defeat of the Iron Belt faction and the recruitment of Lun Liak, now the incumbent Public Orator - Temie was finally elected a consul in 1023, where she could finally begin efforts towards Siayeo confederation. As Consul-Executive, Yeyan Temie has been steadily incrementing the reach and power of the QTB Transnational Assembly.

**Lhoai - Premier Angalke**
Son of a drunkard fishmonger, Huna Angalke enlisted in the river navy of the powerful Heolan River Company in 988, from which he observed the eroding rule of the Chxa Dynasty. His ship was captured by republican insurgents, who recruited him at gunpoint; though a poor footsoldier, he gained a reputation for bravery and was given a battalion to lead against Cehocians in the south. Despite chronic illness inflicted by gas, he continued to command troops and curry political favor - he became the confidante and designated successor of the revolutionary leader Memiau, who was assassinated in 1003 by gunshot. Given freedom to act and pressured by Knebonian and Cehocian attacks, Premier Angalke worked quickly to renormalise Lhoayan QTB membership status, gaining the support of Urwe and Shisha. A great deal of territory was ceded to Cehocoxzin in the peace negotiations, and Angalke gained a reputation for brutality through the strife that continued to batter Lhoayan until his economic reforms in the 1020s.

**Shisha - High Custodian Xa**
Huanui Xa was a child in the slums before being taken in by an Adept of Rains. He engaged in several business ventures, using his parent's connections to speculate on perishables and machinery before founding the government-sponsored Siuon Tucha Company in 1018, an investing and consulting firm with considerable influence over strategic industries. Huanui Xa eventually gained an appointment as a high-ranking economic advisor, from which he propelled his political career towards the position of High Custodian - this he achieved through skillful management of Shisha's incumbent technocrats, many of whom were delivered smoothly into obscurity with the support of the Adepts, existing rivalries, unions, and even foreign cartels. Huanui Xa's reputation reflects this - but he often assures us, "What's good for you is good for me."

**Urwe - Prime Minister Halipamua**
Eaka Halipamua was born into a noble house, enlisting as an officer in the Coalition Navy in 971. Upon her return, she continued her education until her father's retirement from office in 986, at which point she inherited his title. From here she began her political career as a royalist in the House of Lords, gaining a reputation for level-headedness and careful reasoning; she was respected by many in court, and during debates and summits she would often be looked to as a mediator. Following her retirement in 1018 she was retained as an advisor of the queen, over the course of which she perceived numerous political deficiencies and became increasingly wary of foreign revolutions. Eaka returned to the party to run for prime minister, promising to stabilise the Nexus through any means - by guarantee, by concession, or by intervention.

**Yiwayan - President Yishi**
Dadar Yishi was born to an immigrant family from the southwest, entering the civil service after failing the initial bar exam for provincial law. He worked as a clerk, auditor, and finally a commissioner in the Bureau of Agriculture, where his careful handling of the contested land-bond auctions helped cement his appointment as an advisor to the 1013 Fair Land Commission. Though the effort was ultimately stifled, Yishi gained wider recognition, setting the stage for his election as a compromise candidate between the fractured agrindustrial concerns and the industrial unions. He has since succeeded in stemming the growth of the slums, but some of his policies have grown increasingly expansionist; while admired at home, those abroad are divided between his generous QTB contributions and the tensions caused by Yiwayan settlers in vulnerable territories.
