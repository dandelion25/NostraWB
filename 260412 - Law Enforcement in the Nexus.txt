Crime in the Nexus are typically handled by public bodies of investigative career officers that record, respond to and escalate citizen reports within their communities, receiving them either in person, by letter, by telegram, or by telephone. They possess the capacity to detain individuals under certain conditions varying from nation to nation, but most incidents call upon on the officers to mediate disputes, working with citizens, inspecting bodies, and judicial courts to resolve conflicts as deemed appropriate by local and federal laws.

Local law enforcement is typically responsible for patrols and maintaining public order, but in the states of Shisha, Yiwa, Anggayan, and Miayan they may be supplanted in this role by private watchmen or federal entities. Neither are appreciated by local police: the former are often ill-trained and dependent on legal technicalities, while the motivations of the latter can be suspect. This is not unwarranted, as proactive street-level enforcement by any federal body often bodes ill for participants - or in cases, unwitting victims - of what are often highly political operations.

In response to growing economic, and as is inevitable, criminal integration, the Ten Nations Joint Police Bureau was formed in 1009 under the joint control of the QTB nations. Focusing on financial and organised crime, they have successfully halted numerous cases of tax avoidance, smuggling, and mass fraud, providing a vital contribution to the continued harmony of the Nexus.

Rebels, outlaws, and violent criminals are far less common than they once were, but remain a significant problem in regions such as An Ze - some police bodies have invested in improved arsenals and specialised divisions, but often they are able to request support from less judicially oriented, but better-equipped bodies including guards, militia, and even private security forces, which in Shisha are obligated a yearly service of twenty to sixty days under public enforcement bodies.
